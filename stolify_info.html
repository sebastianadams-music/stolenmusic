NOTHING TO SEE HERE COPS

This is a replacement for Spotify hosted on a computer in my house. All the music files are ones downloaded locally onto my NAS (small server in m house) and the music is served via two apps called Navidrome and Feishin. New music can be downloaded using an app called SLSKD.

The only "cloud" provider involved is Cloudflare, which opens tunnels (essentially a form of VPN) so that the music services can be accessed anywhere from simple web addresses, without exposing my network to the internet directly.

> LISTENING TO MUSIC (WEB BROWSER)

The best listening experience in a web browser can be found by going to this web address:

webplay.stolenmusic.org

Then log in using your **personal user details** (first name in lower case + password sent to you privately)

The interface you see here is provided as the app Feishin, hosted via my computer. It is very similar to apps like Spotify.

> OTHER WAYS TO LISTEN

On Android phones there is a fantastic app called Symfonium, which is a front-end for the underlying music library software. It costs 6 euro (one-off), after a free trial.

To log in:

click settings; then "Manage Media Providers", then "Add media provider", then "(Open) Subsonic", then for primary connection enter "stolify.stolenmusic.org" for hostname and "443" for port. Below this, enter your personal login details.

If you think new files haven't been added correctly, you can click the three dots menu beside "Subsonic" and click "Sync".

There are also many other client/front end apps you could use here, if you want to find one, you need to look out for "Open Subsonic" support.

> ADDING MUSIC (SLSKD)

To download music to the server, you need to log in to **SLSKD** by going to the web address:

seeker.stolenmusic.org

And putting in these log-in details:

USERNAME: admin
PASSWORD: PASSWORD SENT SEPARATELY

From there, the interface is a little confusing at first, but you can download music from thousands of other people around the world. This uses a peer-to-peer network called **Soulseek**, which is similar to downloading Torrents except that instead of downloading a file from many people at the same time, you browse search terms and download from individual users' personal collections of files. Many people who use Soulseek are **music buffs** who collect rare recordings and a lot of them are committed to storing them in very good quality.

As a rule of thumb: **MP3 320kbps** is the "best" MP3 format, 128kbps or higher is comparable to normal music streaming platforms, and **FLAC** is a "lossless" compression (which means the audio files are as small as possible without losing any information whatsoever; MP3 is much smaller and at high bit rates it attempts to lose as much information as possible without affecting the listening experience noticeably)

When you click to download (check the files you want, then scroll down and find the download button) they will be put in a download queue and downloaded onto my server. The music library software will **automatically scan** the new files every so often.
